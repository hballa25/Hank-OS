# Book 5 — The AI Homework & Feedback Agent

Grilled seed note (2026-07-07) for [[The Forge — Workbook]] to build from. Part of the [[AI Workbooks]] line; matches the voice/format of [[Book 3 - The Build-an-Agent Workbook]] and Books 1–4. Deliver as **print-ready PDF + Canva** design.

## The concept
A workbook that teaches teachers to **build their own AI agents for administering and giving feedback on homework** — the same territory as [[TaiGrader]] and [[XPScholar]], but done-for-yourself. Not "10 AI tips." A build kit: copy-paste prompts + fill-in pages + starter projects that produce **real, usable classroom tools**.

## Who buys it
Teachers who've outgrown consumer AI-for-teacher tools (e.g. Brisk, MagicSchool) and want something **more powerful and customizable** — they want to *own and shape* the agent, not rent a locked-down one. Sells on TPT (+ Etsy variant later).

## The edge (why it's worth paying for)
- **Copy-paste prompt library** — a big bank of tested prompts for grading, feedback, rubric-building, comment banks, homework generation, and re-teaching.
- **Fill-in workbook pages** — guided exercises that walk the teacher through building the agent for *their own* class/subject.
- **Tied to specific tools** — concrete step-by-step with named tools (ChatGPT / Claude, and a bridge to [[TaiGrader]] / [[XPScholar]]), not abstract theory.
- **Starter projects & examples** — ready seeds teachers can build on (e.g. "the 2-minute feedback agent," "the rubric-to-comments agent").

## Proposed outline (the swarm expands this)
1. **Why build your own** — custom agent vs. locked consumer tools; what becomes possible.
2. **Agent foundations** — the anatomy of a grading/feedback agent (role, rubric, voice, guardrails). Fill-in: define yours.
3. **Prompt library: Homework administration** — generate, differentiate, and assign; copy-paste bank.
4. **Prompt library: Feedback & grading** — rubric → feedback → comment banks; consistency + tone controls.
5. **Starter project A — The 2-Minute Feedback Agent** — build-along + fill-in.
6. **Starter project B — The Rubric-to-Comments Agent** — build-along + fill-in.
7. **Bridge to real tools** — hand off to [[TaiGrader]] / [[XPScholar]] for scale; when to DIY vs. use the app.
8. **Make it yours** — customization patterns, your prompt-tuning worksheet, safety (never put student-identifiable data in a cloud call).
